Archaological sites

pick and choose from our Archaological sites section to explore the historical marvels situated in Bangladesh!

Paharpur

Paharpur is the most spectacular monument in Bangladesh and the second largest single Buddhist monastery of the Himalayas.

Mohasthangar

Mahasthangarh is the oldest archaeological site in Bangladesh. It dates back to 300 BCE and was the ancient capital of the Pundra Kingdom.

Lalbagh Fort

Lalbagh Fort is a fort in the old city of Dhaka, Bangladesh. Its name is derived from its neighborhood Lalbagh, which means Red Garden.

Sonargaon

Sonargaon is a historic city in central Bangladesh. It corresponds to the Sonargaon Upazila of Narayanganj District in Dhaka Division.

Mosque city

The Mosque City of Bagerhat is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Bagerhat District. It contains 360 mosques.

Ahsan Monjil

Ahsan Manzil, aka Pink Palace, was the residence of the most influential family in Dhaka, who were the feudal landlords during British rule.
